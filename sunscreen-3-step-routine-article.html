I found out the hard way, at my sister-in-law's Fourth of July pool party, that "I put sunscreen on this morning" is not the same thing as "I am protected right now." There is a very specific kind of mortification that comes with someone pointing at the sunburn stripe running straight down your part line, in front of your entire in-law family, while you're holding a paper plate of potato salad.

That was three summers ago, back when my sunscreen routine was one bottle, applied once, somewhere around 7:45 a.m., and then forgotten for the rest of the day. If you live anywhere with actual summer humidity — Charleston, in my case — that's basically the same as not wearing sunscreen at all. It slides off. It pills under makeup. And by the time you're sitting in the school pickup line at 2 p.m., whatever SPF you started with has mostly ended up on your steering wheel.

So I stopped looking for one sunscreen to do everything, and started building a system instead: one product for the morning, one for reapplying without wrecking my makeup, and one that can actually survive water. Here's exactly what that looks like, step by step.
